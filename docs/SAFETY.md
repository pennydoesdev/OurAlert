# Safety and Moderation Policy

OurALERT exists to keep communities safer, not to put anyone in harm's way. This document describes the rules that govern what is allowed on the platform, how we moderate it, and how we respond to abuse.

---

## Core principles

1. **People over papers.** Community safety is the entire point. If a feature, policy, or decision puts real people at greater risk, we change it.
2. **Reporters are anonymous by default, forever.** We collect nothing that could identify a person submitting a report. We will never betray that default, even under pressure.
3. **Every report is alleged until verified.** We publish reports only after a volunteer moderator has reviewed them, and even approved reports carry an "Alleged Sighting — Not Confirmed" label until independently confirmed.
4. **We do not tolerate violence, doxxing, or scams.** These undermine the platform's entire reason to exist.

---

## What is allowed

- **Anonymous sighting reports** of ICE, CBP, Border Patrol, or military enforcement activity, with location, time, and description
- **Photos and videos of vehicles, equipment, and uniforms** in publicly observable locations
- **Names of arrestees**, only with the explicit consent of the arrestee or their immediate family, recorded via the consent checkbox on the submission form
- **Neighborhood context** — what happened before, during, or after an enforcement action — to help rapid response networks understand patterns

## What is not allowed

The following content will be rejected in moderation. Repeated attempts to submit it will result in rate-limiting and, where appropriate, reporting to the relevant platform or authority.

### Violence and threats
- Calls for violence against any person, including law enforcement officers
- Threats of physical harm, intimidation, or retaliation
- Content that glorifies or celebrates harm to any person
- Content that could reasonably be interpreted as planning or facilitating violence

### Doxxing and targeting
- Home addresses, personal phone numbers, or family information of any law enforcement officer
- Photos or videos of minors in any context, including as bystanders
- Photos or videos of clearly uninvolved civilians (people who happen to be in the background)
- Information that could be used to target the family of an arrestee for harassment

### Illegal acts
- Content depicting or instructing the commission of any crime other than documenting enforcement activity
- Content that would violate wiretapping or recording laws in the jurisdiction where it was captured
- Stolen material, including body-cam or police communications unless lawfully released

### Scams and fundraising fraud
- Fundraising links not from verified 501(c)(3) legal defense organizations
- Bail-fund scams or fake GoFundMe campaigns
- Impersonation of rapid response networks or legal aid organizations

### Misinformation
- Fabricated sightings or reports the submitter knows to be false
- Recycled photos or videos presented as current events
- Content generated by AI image or video generators presented as real footage

### Hate speech and harassment
- Content attacking any community on the basis of race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, sexual orientation, or immigration status, including communities connected to law enforcement
- Dehumanizing language toward any group

## Moderation workflow

Every report goes through the following flow before it becomes publicly visible:

1. **Submission** — The reporter submits anonymously. The report is created with `moderation_state = 'pending'` and is not visible on the public map.
2. **Automated checks** — EXIF metadata is stripped from photos. A hashed IP is recorded for rate-limiting. The Turnstile token is verified.
3. **Volunteer review** — A trained volunteer moderator opens the report, examines the media, reads the description, checks metadata, and runs a reverse image search if appropriate.
4. **Cross-reference** — Where possible, the moderator cross-references the report with rapid response network channels to confirm consistency.
5. **Decision** — Approve, reject, or flag for senior moderator review. All three decisions are logged in `volunteer_actions` with the moderator's ID, timestamp, and any notes.
6. **Publication** — Approved reports appear on the public map with a "Not Confirmed" badge. Fan-out cron jobs trigger email alerts and push notifications to subscribers within the zip radius.
7. **Post-publication** — Reports can be re-reviewed and taken down at any time. The audit log preserves the moderation history.

## Volunteer code of conduct

All volunteer moderators must:

- Complete onboarding before being given access to the moderation queue
- Use two-factor authentication for every login
- Never share their login credentials
- Never use the platform to collect, store, or redistribute personally identifiable information outside the platform's normal flow
- Recuse themselves from moderating reports involving people or organizations they have a personal relationship with
- Report any attempt to bribe, pressure, or coerce them to `security@ouralert.org`
- Treat the moderation queue as confidential — unpublished reports are not for sharing outside the platform

Volunteers who violate the code of conduct will have their access suspended pending review.

## Takedown process

If you are the subject of a report, the family of the subject, or a legal representative, you can request removal by emailing `takedown@ouralert.org`. Include:

- The URL of the specific report
- Your relationship to the subject (reporter, subject, family member, attorney)
- A brief explanation of why removal is requested

We review takedown requests within **72 hours**. Valid reasons for takedown include:

- The subject or their family withdraws consent to publish identifying information
- The report contains factual errors that cannot be corrected in-place
- The report was submitted in bad faith or by someone without standing
- Continued publication would create a specific, identifiable safety risk to the subject

We do not honor takedown requests from law enforcement agencies except in response to a valid court order. We will publish a transparency report annually listing the number of such requests received and our responses, subject to any applicable gag orders.

---

## Law enforcement requests

We cooperate with lawfully issued, narrowly scoped court orders. We do not cooperate with informal requests, "please remove this" emails, or threats of legal action without a court order.

Because we collect almost no personally identifiable information, there is very little for us to turn over even under a lawful order. Our data retention defaults are:

- Raw IP hashes: 7 days
- Raw analytics events: 30 days
- Report media: retained indefinitely unless taken down
- Report metadata: retained indefinitely unless taken down
- Volunteer accounts: retained while active, anonymized action logs kept for audit

We publish a transparency report annually.

---

## Reporting abuse

To report a violation of this policy, email `abuse@ouralert.org` with:

- The URL of the report or account involved
- A description of the violation
- Any relevant screenshots or context

We review abuse reports within 48 hours and will notify you of the outcome where possible.

---

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the platform grows. Material changes will be noted at the top of this document and, where relevant, communicated to volunteer moderators directly. We will never lower the safety protections described here without volunteer consultation.
